Bike Trailer

This monstrosity was bourn out of various parts scavenged from a fraternity house, the desire to carry musical instruments to campus without using a motorized vehicle, and the sheer joy of carrying ridiculous things on a bicycle. There's a special feeling to thundering down the bike path with a pair of guitars in tow. Now for some spefications. I own a guitar and an electric keyboard. I desired to carry either of these instruments via bicycle without paying a single cent to do so.

The Supplies
- Pair of Orbit Wheels*
- Various 2x lumber scraps, with a 2x6 for the primary beam
- A couple feet of bungee cord**
- A foot of utility cord
- Various Screws
- A small piece of oak for mounting hardware to the bike rack
*Note that these were in the cave (the room at Pike that I lived in) for at least 4 years, and that of the dozens of people who tried, none were able to conquer the orbit wheels. I would venture to say that they are a cool concept but unusable except for the rare early 2000's child prodigy. I didn't feel too bad for dedicating them to the cause, though I did not damage them irreparably - only 4 small holes in the platform. Check them out for yourself. **This particular bit of bungee cord was found on the top of a cliff in devil's lake wisconsin. Still scratching my head about the utility of bungee cord on the top of a cliff, but happy to use it for this project nontheless.

I'm not particularly proud of the design of this contraption, nor do I want anyone to replicate it, so I won't go into the details of its creation. Just wanted to share a project that brought me unprecedented joy and a trivial amount of transportation freedom from very little time and resources. If you have similar ambitions, I encourage you to go for it! I was able to carry my electric keyboard, or my guitar, or my roomate Caleb's bass guitar, or both guitars (pictured)

Full disclaimer: One iteration of the attachment/hitch mechanism was not well thought out and resulted in my dropping the trailer laden with my electric keyboard after hitting a bump, rendering the hitch mechanism useless. The keyboard was unaffected, though my pride greviously injured. I have employed the most recent iteration of the hitch mechanism numerous occasions without fail. It involves a loop of utility cord attached to the trailer resting in a metal hook on the hitch. If you do undertake a trailer design, I admonish you not to overlook the signifiicance of a sound hitch design :).

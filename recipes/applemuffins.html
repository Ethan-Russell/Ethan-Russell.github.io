Apple Muffins

Good recipe for using up apples that are getting soft, or just any ole apple

Ingredients
- 4 small-medium apples or equivalent, chopped
- ¼ brown sugar
- 2 tsp cinnamon
- ¼ tsp allspice
- ¼ tsp nutmeg
- 1 ⅔ cup flour (I used King Arthur Gluten Free, but you could probably use glutinous flour too)
- 1 tsp salt
- 2 tsp baking powder
- ¼ cup oil
- 2 eggs
- Apple cider or water, as needed, ~¼ c
Instructions
- Dice the apples, put in a saucepan with the cider and spices over medium heat. Let this cook, covered for a few minutes, stirring occasionally. You want them to get soft, but not quite applesauce consistency - you want recognizable pieces of soft apple floating around in the muffins.
- While the apples are cooking, mix together flour, salt, and baking powder in a big bowl
- Separately beat the eggs and oil
- Oil muffin tins
- Once the apples have cooked and cooled a bit (you can use an ice bath to cool it faster if you're impatient), dump everything together, and stir it all together, using as few stirring strokes as possible. Add water/cider if needed to get muffin batter consistency.
- Spoon into muffin tins and bake at 375 until they look done, ~20-24 minutes minutes. Enjoy!
